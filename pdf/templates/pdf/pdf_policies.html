1. Accounting, Financial & Reporting Policies

1.1 Books and Records Keeping Policy

Purpose: To ensure accurate, complete, and reliable recording and maintenance of financial transactions in compliance with applicable laws and accounting standards.

  1. All financial transactions must be promptly, accurately, and completely recorded in Ozone's books and records.
  2. The books and records must reflect the true nature of transactions, supported by appropriate documentation and evidence.
  3. Falsification, alteration, or unauthorized destruction of records is strictly prohibited.
  4. All transactions must be recorded in a timely manner and properly classified to the appropriate accounts.
  5. Regular reconciliations of bank accounts, ledgers, and subsidiary records should be performed to ensure accuracy.

1.2 Authority to Approve Payments Policy

Purpose: To define the authorization process for approving payments and ensure proper controls, accountability, and compliance.

  1. Payments should only be made based on valid and authorized documents in accordance with established policies.
  2. Only authorized individuals with appropriate levels of authority should approve payments.
  3. The approval process must ensure appropriate segregation of duties and prevent conflicts of interest.
  4. A clear and documented approval hierarchy must outline authority levels required for various payment amounts.
  5. A proper documentation trail — including invoices, purchase orders, expense reports — must support all payment authorizations.

1.3 Documents Retention Policy

Purpose: To outline requirements for retaining and disposing of financial documents in compliance with legal, regulatory, and internal control requirements.

  1. Ozone will retain financial documents for periods specified by applicable laws, regulations, and industry best practices.
  2. Documents should be stored securely and be readily accessible for audit, legal, and regulatory purposes.
  3. Proper disposal methods must be employed to protect sensitive information and maintain data privacy.
  4. A centralized and secure document management system must be maintained to organize financial records.

All employees are expected to familiarize themselves with these policies and seek guidance from the appropriate authority in case of any questions or concerns.

2. Anti-Bribery and Corruption Policy

Ozone is committed to conducting its business with the highest ethical standards and complying with all applicable laws and regulations. This policy establishes our zero-tolerance stance against bribery and corruption.

2.1 Policy Statement

  1. Ozone strictly prohibits bribery and corruption in all its forms, whether committed by employees, contractors, agents, or any other party acting on our behalf.
  2. We adhere to all relevant anti-bribery and corruption laws, including the EFCC, NAFDAC, SON, and applicable international legislation.
  3. Any violation of this policy will result in disciplinary action, which may include termination of employment or legal action.

2.2 Prohibited Activities

  1. Offering, giving, receiving, or soliciting bribes, kickbacks, or improper payments in cash, gifts, entertainment, or any other form.
  2. Making facilitation payments to expedite routine government actions.
  3. Engaging in transactions intended to conceal the true nature of payments.
  4. Participating in any activity that could create an actual or perceived conflict of interest.
  5. Failing to maintain accurate books, records, and accounts that reflect the company's transactions.

2.3 Reporting & Whistleblowing

  1. Employees, contractors, and stakeholders are encouraged to report any suspected instances of bribery or corruption without fear of reprisal.
  2. An anonymous reporting mechanism is established to facilitate confidential reporting.
  3. All reported or suspected instances will be promptly investigated in a fair, thorough, and confidential manner.
  4. Appropriate disciplinary action will be taken against individuals found to be involved.

3. Compliance Roles, Policies & Procedures

3.1 Compliance Manager

The Compliance Manager is responsible for overseeing the compliance function, ensuring adherence to all relevant laws, regulations, and internal policies, and reporting to senior management.

3.2 Compliance Policies and Procedures

The compliance function is responsible for developing, implementing, and maintaining comprehensive compliance policies and procedures that align with applicable laws and regulations.

3.3 Risk Assessment and Monitoring

  1. The compliance team conducts regular risk assessments to identify potential compliance risks and vulnerabilities.
  2. Monitoring programmes are designed and implemented to detect and mitigate compliance breaches using data analytics, audits, and internal controls.

3.4 Training and Awareness

  1. Training programmes are designed and delivered to educate employees on their compliance obligations and promote a culture of ethics and integrity.
  2. Awareness campaigns communicate updates in laws and regulations and provide ongoing guidance to employees.

3.5 Investigations and Reporting

  1. The compliance team investigates all reported or suspected compliance breaches, misconduct, or unethical behaviour.
  2. A process is maintained for reporting compliance concerns, including an anonymous reporting mechanism.
  3. Appropriate action is taken in response to all substantiated allegations.

3.6 Regulatory Compliance

The compliance function monitors and ensures compliance with relevant laws, regulations, and industry standards. Processes are established to keep abreast of changes in regulatory requirements and implement necessary adjustments.

This document is confidential and intended solely for Ozone F & L employees and authorised stakeholders.
The specific roles and structure of the compliance function may be customised to meet organisational needs.
