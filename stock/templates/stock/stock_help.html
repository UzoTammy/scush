Getting Around the Stock Management Page

From Stock Management, the top bar groups every tool into three menus:

  • Reports — Stock Report, All Products, Performance, Run Rate, and Stock Balancing (reorder suggestions).
  • Operations — day-to-day stock work: Deliveries, Watchlist, Sellout & Balance reporting, Stock Count, Stock Count History, and Stock Transfer.
  • PDF — printable price lists and price-update notes by source.

Every individual product also has a Stock Card link on its detail page — this is where you see and adjust that product's running stock balance.

Stock Card & the Movement Ledger

Every stock change for a product — receipts, sales, returns, transfers, adjustments and write-offs — is recorded as a Stock Movement. The Stock Card (open it from a product's detail page) shows:

  • Ledger Balance — the running total of all movements. This is the system's "true" stock figure.
  • Reported Stock — the most recent figure entered through the daily/weekly stock report (for comparison).
  • A breakdown of the balance at each stock location, if you use more than one.
  • A form to record a new movement, and a history table of everything recorded so far.

Recording a movement manually: choose a movement type (Opening Balance, Receipt, Sale, Return, Transfer, Adjustment or Write-off), enter the quantity — use a negative number to reduce stock (e.g. a sale or write-off) and a positive number to increase it (e.g. a receipt) — pick a date, optionally a location, a reference (PO number, delivery note, etc.) and a note, then save.

Deliveries & Automatic Stock Updates

A delivery moves through five stages on the Delivery Management home page: Created → Delivered → Returned → Confirmed → Credited. Click any stage tile to see the deliveries currently sitting at that stage.

When a delivery is moved to the Returned stage (i.e. the outlet has confirmed what was delivered vs. received), the system automatically creates Stock Movements for you: a Sale movement for the quantity delivered, and — if anything was sent back — a matching Return movement for the quantity credited. You don't need to update the Stock Card separately for deliveries; this happens once per delivery, so resubmitting the same return won't double-count it.

Stock Count (Physical Count)

Use Stock Count when you physically count what's on the shelves/in the warehouse and want to reconcile it with the system's ledger balance.

  1. Set the count date and an optional note.
  2. Products are grouped into tabs by source — switch tabs and enter the counted quantity for each product you checked. Leave a product blank to skip it.
  3. Submit. The system compares your counted quantity to the ledger balance for each product.
  4. Any difference (variance) is automatically recorded as an Adjustment stock movement, so the ledger balance matches what you counted — giving you a clean audit trail of shrinkage or overages.

Past counts — including the variance for every line — can be reviewed from Stock Count History.

Reorder Suggestions & Low Stock

Each product can have a Reorder Point, Reorder Quantity, Minimum Stock Level and Maximum Stock Level set on its edit page. These drive two things:

  • The Stock Balancing page lists products that need reordering (at or below their reorder point, with a suggested order quantity and the supplier's lead time), products that are overstocked (at or above their maximum level), and products that have no levels configured yet.
  • The Performance dashboard's "Low Stock" widget flags products whose reported balance has fallen to or below their reorder point (or minimum stock level if no reorder point is set).

If a product shows under "Needs Configuration", open it from Stock Management and set its stock levels via Update Product so it can be tracked properly.

Stock Transfer Between Locations

If stock is held in more than one place (e.g. a main warehouse and a depot), set up each place as a Stock Location in App Settings.

Use Stock Transfer to move stock from one location to another: pick the product, quantity, source and destination locations, and a date. This creates two linked movements (a deduction at the source location and an addition at the destination), so the total company-wide balance is unchanged — only the per-location split moves. The Stock Card for each product shows its balance broken down by location.

Settings: Categories, Suppliers & Locations

The Products tab of App Settings is where the building blocks of the stock system are managed:

  • Product Categories — group products (Malt, Lager, Stout, etc.) for reporting.
  • Product Sources — also act as suppliers. Click the icon next to a source to record its contact person, phone, email and typical lead time (used on the Reorder Suggestions page).
  • Stock Locations — the physical places stock is held, used for Stock Transfers and per-location balances on the Stock Card.
