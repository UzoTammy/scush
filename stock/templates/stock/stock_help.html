Getting Around the Stock Management Page

Stock Management is the home base for this section. Its top bar groups every tool into three menus, plus two direct links:

  • Stock Reports — Stock Report (all products, grouped by source), Performance (the daily performance dashboard), Run Rate (sellout velocity and days of cover), and Stock Balancing (reorder suggestions).
  • Products — All Products (browse/search every product), Watchlist, and New Product (create a product).
  • Inventory Operations — day-to-day stock work: Sellout & Balance reporting, Stock Count, Stock Transfer, and Deliveries.
  • Price Update — quickly increase or decrease a single product's selling price.

Every individual product also has a Stock Card link on its detail page — this is where you see and adjust that product's running stock balance.

The Stock Management Dashboard

The cards at the top of Stock Management summarise current stock:

  • Current Stock Value — the total value of stock on hand, valued at cost price.
  • Total Quantity — total units currently in stock across all active products.
  • Quantity Received — total units received through Deliveries.
  • Quantity Transferred — total units moved between stock locations via Stock Transfer.

Top Ten Sellout Stocks — the ten products with the biggest movement over the last 3 days (4 if the period spans a weekend), ranked by the size of their profit or loss — so both your best earners and your biggest loss-makers show up here. Losses are shown in red with a leading minus sign (e.g. -N53,650.27).

Price Changes — every price increase or decrease made this month, who made it, and the old vs. new price. Use this to audit recent price activity.

Performance Dashboard

Performance gives a daily snapshot of how stock is moving, as at the most recent date with recorded figures. The KPI row at the top shows:

  • Active Products — how many products are currently active and tracked.
  • Availability — the percentage of active products that currently have stock on hand (i.e. are not at zero/negative balance). A low percentage means many products are out of stock.
  • Sales Activity — of the products currently in stock, what percentage sold at least one unit today. A low percentage means stock is sitting still even though it's available.
  • Products Sold — how many products recorded a sale today. Click the number to open the Products Sold - Yield breakdown (sellout quantity, selling price, sales amount, stock value and profit per product, with totals).
  • Profit From Sales — total profit earned from today's sales across all products.
  • LDC (Locked Down Capital) — the value of stock that has not sold a single unit in the last month. This is cash tied up in slow-moving stock.

Below the KPIs, four cards highlight top performers for Today / This Month / Year-to-Date:

  • Most Sold - Quantity — the product with the highest sellout quantity.
  • Most Profitable — the product that generated the most profit.
  • Today's Margin — the product with the best and worst margin today, plus the average margin across all sales. A worst margin shown in red with ** means that product was sold below cost price (a loss).
  • Highest Gross Sales — the product with the highest total sales amount (before deducting cost).

The Stock & Sellout Watch table flags products needing attention, both for today and for the current month — click any number to see the product list:

  • No Stock — products with a zero or negative balance.
  • Low Stock — products at or below their reorder point (see Reorder Suggestions).
  • No Sellout — products in stock that recorded no sales. The daily view is sorted by days since last sale (longest first).
  • Low Sellout — products in stock with sellout below their expected level.
Run Rate & Product Analysis

The Run Rate page (Product Analysis) helps you understand how fast stock is selling and how long it will last:

  • Product Velocity - Sellout — every active product is bucketed by sellout speed (Not Designated, Not Selling, Very Low, Low, Moderate, High, Very High), showing the stock value and quantity in each bucket. Click a quantity to see which products are in that bucket.
  • Run Rate & Days of Cover — for each product, the average daily sellout over the last 7 and 30 days, the current balance, the resulting days of cover (how many days the current stock will last at the recent sellout rate), and a status badge:
    • Low — days of cover is below the safe threshold; consider reordering.
    • Overstock — stock is well above what's needed; consider slowing down orders.
    • OK — stock levels are healthy.
    • Unset — stock levels haven't been configured for this product yet.
  • Products Not Sold — products in stock that haven't sold in 7 days or more, with their current stock and when/how much they last sold.
Stock Card & the Movement Ledger

Every stock change for a product — receipts, sales, returns, transfers, adjustments and write-offs — is recorded as a Stock Movement. The Stock Card (open it from a product's detail page) shows:

  • Ledger Balance — the running total of all movements. This is the system's "true" stock figure.
  • Reported Stock — the most recent figure entered through the daily/weekly stock report (for comparison).
  • A breakdown of the balance at each stock location, if you use more than one.
  • A form to record a new movement, and a history table of everything recorded so far.

Recording a movement manually: choose a movement type (Opening Balance, Receipt, Sale, Return, Transfer, Adjustment or Write-off), enter the quantity — use a negative number to reduce stock (e.g. a sale or write-off) and a positive number to increase it (e.g. a receipt) — pick a date, optionally a location, a reference (PO number, delivery note, etc.) and a note, then save.

Deliveries & Automatic Stock Updates

A delivery moves through five stages on the Delivery Management home page: Created → Delivered → Returned → Confirmed → Credited. Click any stage tile to see the deliveries currently sitting at that stage.

When a delivery is moved to the Returned stage (i.e. the outlet has confirmed what was delivered vs. received), the system automatically creates Stock Movements for you: a Sale movement for the quantity delivered, and — if anything was sent back — a matching Return movement for the quantity credited. You don't need to update the Stock Card separately for deliveries; this happens once per delivery, so resubmitting the same return won't double-count it.

Stock Count (Physical Count)

Use Stock Count when you physically count what's on the shelves/in the warehouse and want to reconcile it with the system's ledger balance.

  1. Set the count date and an optional note.
  2. Products are grouped into tabs by source — switch tabs and enter the counted quantity for each product you checked. Leave a product blank to skip it.
  3. Submit. The system compares your counted quantity to the ledger balance for each product.
  4. Any difference (variance) is automatically recorded as an Adjustment stock movement, so the ledger balance matches what you counted — giving you a clean audit trail of shrinkage or overages.

Past counts — including the variance for every line — can be reviewed from Stock Count History.

Reorder Suggestions & Low Stock

Each product can have a Reorder Point, Reorder Quantity, Minimum Stock Level and Maximum Stock Level set on its edit page. These drive two things:

  • The Stock Balancing page lists products that need reordering (at or below their reorder point, with a suggested order quantity and the supplier's lead time), products that are overstocked (at or above their maximum level), and products that have no levels configured yet.
  • The Performance dashboard's "Low Stock" row in the Stock & Sellout Watch table flags products whose reported balance has fallen to or below their reorder point (or minimum stock level if no reorder point is set).

If a product shows under "Needs Configuration", open it from Stock Management and set its stock levels via Update Product so it can be tracked properly.

Stock Transfer Between Locations

If stock is held in more than one place (e.g. a main warehouse and a depot), set up each place as a Stock Location in App Settings.

Use Stock Transfer to move stock from one location to another: pick the product, quantity, source and destination locations, and a date. This creates two linked movements (a deduction at the source location and an addition at the destination), so the total company-wide balance is unchanged — only the per-location split moves. The Stock Card for each product shows its balance broken down by location.

Goods Received

Use Goods Received to record a supplier delivery covering several products at once. Pick a date, optional receiving location and reference (e.g. an invoice or PO number), then enter the quantity received for each product — leave a product blank to skip it. Each entry is saved as a stock receipt, increasing that product's balance, and shows up in the Recent Receipts list and the product's Stock Card.

Prices & Price History
  • Price Update — select a product, see its current selling price, then increase or decrease it by an amount. The preview shows the new price before you save.
  • Price List — browse the cost and selling price of every product.
  • Price History — open from a product's detail page to see every past price change for that product, who made it and when.

Every price change recorded here also appears in the Price Changes card on the Stock Management dashboard for the current month.

Watchlist

The Watchlist is a shortlist of products you want to keep a closer eye on (e.g. new arrivals, problem stock, fast movers). Add or remove products from the Watchlist page itself.

Settings: Categories, Sources & Locations

The Products tab of App Settings is where the building blocks of the stock system are managed:

  • Product Categories — group products (Malt, Lager, Stout, etc.) for reporting.
  • Product Sources — also act as suppliers. Click the icon next to a source to record its contact person, phone, email and typical lead time (used on the Reorder Suggestions page).
  • Stock Locations — the physical places stock is held, used for Stock Transfers and per-location balances on the Stock Card.
