Accounting, Financial & Reporting Policies
  1. Books and Records Keeping Policy
    • All financial transactions must be promptly, accurately, and completely recorded in Ozone's books and records.
    • The books and records must reflect the true nature of transactions, supported by appropriate documentation.
    • Falsification, alteration, or unauthorized destruction of records is strictly prohibited.
  2. Authority to Approve Payments Policy
    • Payments should only be made based on valid and authorized documents.
    • Only authorized individuals with appropriate levels of authority should approve payments.
    • The approval process should ensure appropriate segregation of duties.
  3. Documents Retention Policy
    • Ozone will retain financial documents for periods specified by applicable laws and regulations.
    • Documents should be stored securely and be readily accessible for audit, legal, and regulatory purposes.
    • Proper disposal methods should be employed to protect sensitive information.
Anti-Bribery and Corruption Policy
  1. Introduction

    Ozone is committed to conducting its business with the highest ethical standards. This policy establishes our stance against bribery and corruption.

  2. Policy Statement
    • Ozone strictly prohibits bribery and corruption in all its forms.
    • We maintain a zero-tolerance approach; any violation will result in disciplinary action.
  3. Prohibited Activities
    • Offering, giving, receiving, or soliciting bribes, kickbacks, or improper payments.
    • Making facilitation payments to expedite routine government actions.
    • Participating in any activity that could create an actual or perceived conflict of interest.
  4. Reporting & Whistleblowing
    • Employees, contractors, and stakeholders are encouraged to report any suspected instances of bribery or corruption without fear of reprisal.
    • An anonymous reporting mechanism is available to facilitate reporting.
Compliance Roles, Policies & Procedures
  1. Compliance Manager
    • Responsible for overseeing the compliance function and ensuring adherence to all relevant laws, regulations, and internal policies.
  2. Compliance Policies and Procedures
    • The compliance function develops, implements, and maintains comprehensive compliance policies aligned with applicable laws and regulations.
  3. Risk Assessment and Monitoring
    • The compliance team conducts regular risk assessments to identify potential compliance risks and vulnerabilities.
  4. Training and Awareness
    • Training programs are designed and delivered to educate employees on their compliance obligations and promote a culture of ethics and integrity.
